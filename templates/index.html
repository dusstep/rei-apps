Financial Metrics Legend

  • NOI:NOI (Net Operating Income): The total income from the property after subtracting operating expenses (but not including debt service costs). This indicates the cash flow from the property before financing.
  • Cap Rate:Cap Rate (Capitalization Rate): The rate of return based on the property’s income. It’s calculated as NOI divided by the property’s purchase price, indicating the expected return on investment.
  • GRM:GRM (Gross Rent Multiplier): A simple valuation metric calculated as the property’s purchase price divided by its annual rental income. This metric provides a basic comparison of rental properties.
  • Cash-on-Cash:Cash-on-Cash Return: The rate of return on the actual cash investment made. It’s calculated by dividing NOI by the initial cash investment, giving a percentage that represents ROI for cash invested.
  • LTV Ratio:LTV Ratio (Loan-to-Value Ratio): The percentage of the property’s value that’s financed by a loan. It’s calculated by dividing the loan amount by the property’s purchase price, showing the amount of leverage used.
